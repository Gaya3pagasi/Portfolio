Character Design

A spear-wielding cat-hybrid hero for a fantasy mobile game — designed as a full character sheet, then distilled down into a silhouette, a 64×64 UI icon, and a themed play button so the character carries the whole game's visual identity.

Role

Character & UI Illustrator

Year

2026

Deliverables

Character Sheet, Silhouette, App Icon, Play Button

The brief was to design a playable hero for a fantasy mobile game — a human-animal hybrid character who reads instantly as the game's mascot, even when scaled down to a 64px icon on a phone screen. I designed a young cat-hybrid spearman: sharp violet hair, tufted ears, a long striped tail, and a travel-worn cloak over a hunter's outfit, carrying a jeweled spear as his signature weapon.

Rather than stopping at a single illustration, I treated the character as a small design system. The full-color character sheet establishes his silhouette, palette, and gear. From there, I stripped him down to a pure black silhouette — a quick test to make sure the design still reads clearly by shape alone, which matters for in-game recognition at a glance and for loading/placeholder states.

That same silhouette-first thinking carried into the UI work. The character was cropped and simplified into a 64×64 profile icon for menus and player cards, and his color palette — deep purple, warm brown leather, and a glowing amber accent — was pulled directly into a matching "Play" button, so the button feels like it belongs to the same world as the character rather than being a generic UI element dropped on top.

The result is a single character concept that scales cleanly across every surface it needs to live on: full illustration, silhouette, tiny icon, and interactive button — all sharing one consistent visual language.
